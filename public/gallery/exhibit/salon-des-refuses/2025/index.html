Right Identity

What would it be like to just be accepted for who you are—without explanation, without justification? Cisgender people don't have to explain themselves, nor do they even consider the need to explain themselves. They know who they are and who everyone else is. But being cisgender is not the only possibility, it's simply the most common. Instead of celebrating diversity and the uniqueness that every individual brings to our human family, ideologies inspired by fear motivate policies that further exclude, remove protections, and even intentionally harm those that don't fit the mold. This piece was created in response to anti-trans legislation in 2024 (not the first nor the last). When politicians focus on creating enemies where there are none—all while real needs are being left unresolved—we know that we have all failed. Identity is known and discovered by the person themselves, not some ideology, not some arbitrary set of rules. The least we can do for each other is respect everyone's personhood and identity. That's a really low bar.

Non-Binary

A non-binary gender identity is complex and beautiful. In this piece, a subtle, abstract face is drawn blind-contour style in the middle of two other faces, representing the internal identity between the two more extreme gender expressions. There is no binary, just a gradient of possible expressions and identities that further highlights the expansive horizon of human experience.

I wasn't a stranger

A critique of white Christian nationalism and abusive ICE policies and practices. This charcoal piece depicts a person behind a literal and metaphorical chainlink fence—crucifix shapes appear at various intersections of the fence pattern. Their eyes are slashed out with dark strokes, suggesting the lack of identity or dignity.
